1What this is

A vision-required prompt template that reads your adjusted sales grid (from a PDF or a screenshot), classifies each comparable correctly, suggests reconciliation weights with reasoning, and drafts the reconciliation narrative.

The critical move it gets right that many appraisers + reviewers get wrong: only closed sales get reconciliation weight. Pendings and active listings support the value (market direction, upper bound) but don't anchor the reconciled value. Weights sum to 100% across closed sales only.

🟢 Closed sales
Get weighted. Weights sum to 100%.
🟡 Pending
Support market direction. NOT weighted.
🔴 Active listings
Support upper bound. NOT weighted.

The AI reads the grid carefully — comp status indicators (s = sold/closed, p = pending, list dates without close dates = active), date formats, contract vs settlement dates — and tells you what it found before suggesting weights, so you can verify it read the grid correctly.

2What you give it

Required: one of these, attached to the chat:

  • A PDF of the completed appraisal (the AI finds the sales grid inside)
  • OR a screenshot / image of just the sales grid (the AI reads it as-is)

Optional — type these in chat under the prompt if you want them reflected:

  • Indicated Value by Cost Approach (or "not developed" / "not required for VA")
  • Indicated Value by Income Approach (or "not developed")
  • Your own weight preferences (e.g., "I'm leaning 35% on Comp 1, 35% on Comp 3, 30% on Comp 2 — please draft the narrative around that")
  • Type of value: as-is / subject-to-completion / subject-to-repairs
  • Final reconciled value if you've already decided it (the AI will draft around your number)
The AI's suggested weights are starting points. You — the appraiser — own the final weighting decision. The tool's job is to draft defensible reasoning and write the narrative. Adjust weights and re-prompt if you disagree.

3How to use it

  1. Click Copy prompt to clipboard above.
  2. Open Claude.ai or ChatGPT.
  3. Paste the prompt.
  4. Attach a PDF of the report (best — the AI sees the full context) OR paste a screenshot of just the grid (faster — works if you don't want to share the full report).
  5. Optionally type your other inputs below the prompt (Cost/Income values, your preferred weights, type of value, etc.).
  6. Send. Wait 30–90 seconds.
  7. Verify the comp-status classifications. Adjust weights if needed. Paste the narrative into TOTAL.

## Suggested Weights

Weights total 100% across closed sales only (Comp 5 is an active listing — no weight).

- **Comp 2 — 35%** — Closest match to subject by GLA, basement configuration, and quality rating. Lowest gross adjustment (3.7%) of any comp; highest reliability. Recent sale (04/2025) within the relevant window.
- **Comp 1 — 30%** — Same neighborhood, similar age, comparable basement size. Moderate gross adjustment (17.3%) primarily from quality. Sale date 09/2025 — most recent closed sale.
- **Comp 4 — 20%** — Similar quality and condition; larger lot adjusted appropriately. Sale date 08/2025. Bracketing-supportive on the higher end of the range.
- **Comp 3 — 15%** — Largest gross adjustment (26.1%) reduces reliability; useful for bracketing the lower end but receives the least weight.

Comp 5 (active listing) is excluded from weighting per Fannie Mae and standard reconciliation practice. It is referenced in the narrative as support for the upper bound of value.
