Comp Verification

Draft professional verification emails to listing agents — one per agent — to confirm concessions, compensation, multiple offers, relationships, and close price/date on your comps.

← All tools

1What this is

A ready-to-use prompt that turns your comp list into polished verification emails to the listing agents. Paste your comps (or attach an MLS export) and the AI drafts one email per agent — combining multiple comps from the same agent into a single message so nobody gets emailed twice. Each email asks the same set of verification questions appraisers need answered under today's MLS concession/compensation rules.

It runs in any AI chat you already use. Nothing is uploaded to us, no signup, no data leaves your screen.

Copy the prompt

Paste it into Claude or ChatGPT, then give it your comps.

2How to use it

  1. Copy the prompt above and paste it into a new chat in Claude or ChatGPT.
  2. Give it your comps. Paste your MLS data, attach an MLS export PDF, or just type a list — each comp's address plus the listing agent's name and email.
  3. Add your signature once. Tell it your name, company, phone, and coverage area so every email is signed correctly (or it will leave a fill-in placeholder).
  4. Review and send. It returns one ready-to-send email per agent. Copy each into your email client, give it a final read, and send.
  5. Acknowledge replies. When an agent answers, the prompt includes a short thank-you you can send back on the same thread.

3Which AI to use

Claude (claude.ai)

Handles attached MLS PDFs well and keeps the email tone consistent. A good default.

ChatGPT

Works just as well for pasted comp lists. Use whichever you already have open.

4The questions it asks each agent

  • Were there any concessions paid, and if so, how much and who paid them?
  • Did the concessions affect the final sales price (was the price raised to cover them)?
  • Did the seller contribute to the buyer's agent compensation?
  • If so, was the purchase price increased to cover that compensation — and by how much?
  • Were there multiple offers, and were they below, at, or above asking?
  • Did multiple offers push the final contract price above list?
  • Were the buyer and seller related?
  • Can they confirm the close date and close price against county records?

This is a documentation aid, not a substitute for your professional judgment. Verify and weigh every response yourself — USPAP requires reasonable verification, and what counts as reasonable is your call.
