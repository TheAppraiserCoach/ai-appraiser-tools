1What this is

A voice agent is an AI that answers your business phone. When you can't pick up — you're on an inspection, driving, or just heads-down — it greets the caller, answers the questions you'd normally answer (turn times, fees, coverage area, what's needed to get started), captures their info, and offers to pass them through to you. It's the same idea as a self-checkout: faster for the caller, and it takes the routine calls off your plate.

This page gives you two prompts: one that walks you through building the agent on the platform of your choice, and a ready-to-edit script for the agent itself. Both are free. You bring your own AI chat (Claude or ChatGPT) and a voice platform.

2The build, in plain steps

However you build it, the steps are the same — only the buttons differ between platforms:

  1. Pick a platform (see options below) and create an account.
  2. Get a phone number — buy one in the platform, or point an existing number to it.
  3. Write the greeting — exactly how it answers every call. Disclose it's an AI right up front (more on that below).
  4. Write the agent's prompt / "goals" — this is the most important part. It's the agent's personality and rules. Use the ready-made script below as your starting point, then have Claude/ChatGPT tailor it.
  5. Build a knowledge base — feed it what it should know: crawl your website, upload an FAQ doc, add your hours/fees/coverage area. You can keep separate knowledge bases (e.g. one for customers, one for staff questions).
  6. Pick and tune the voice — most platforms use ElevenLabs voices. Slow it to ~90% for a more natural pace, and raise "interrupt sensitivity" so it stops talking the moment the caller speaks.
  7. Set a max call length — caps your cost so nobody can run up tokens with an hour-long call.
  8. Attach the phone number, then call it and test. Adjust the prompt and voice until it feels right.
  9. Watch the call logs to see what's being asked and keep improving the knowledge base.

Tip from the session: punctuation controls pacing — a comma makes it pause, a period makes a longer stop. If it sounds rushed or robotic, check the punctuation in your greeting and prompt. And when you're stuck, screenshot the screen into Claude and ask why it sounds off.

3Be honest that it's a bot

The strong recommendation from the session: lead with transparency. People don't mind talking to an AI — they mind being tricked. Have the agent introduce itself as an AI assistant in its first sentence ("Hi, this is [Name], an AI assistant for [Company]…") and always give callers an easy way to reach a human. It sets the tone, avoids annoyance, and it's just the right thing to do.

5Where to build it

  
You don't need any one product — these all do the same job. Pick whatever fits your budget and comfort:

  

    
Go High Level
An all-in-one CRM that bundles the phone number, voice, and AI in one place — easiest if you want everything connected. Has ongoing per-minute + monthly costs.

    
Synthflow
The most popular pick for a standalone voice agent without a full CRM. Beginner-friendly.

    
Retell AI
Quick to set up a solid voice agent on a single number.

    
Vapi
More developer-oriented; great if you (or a helper) are comfortable with technical setup.

    
Bland AI
Built for call centers and large scale, but works fine on one number too.

    
Roll your own
Go straight to ElevenLabs (voice) + Twilio (phone number) and connect them with n8n or Zapier. Most control, lowest middleman cost.

  

  
Expect ongoing costs no matter the platform: a monthly fee, a per-minute phone charge, and AI/voice tokens per call. For typical low call volume it's usually modest — but it's not a flat fee, so check the pricing before you scale.
